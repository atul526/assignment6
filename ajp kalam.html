In A Memory of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

A.P.J Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam,(born October 15,1932, Rameswaram, India

His father Jainulabdeen was a boat owner and imam of local mosque, his mother Ashiamma was a housewife.

Education

In his school years, Kalam had averge grades but was described as a bright and hardworking student. 1952 he graduated in Physics. 1955 he Study in Aerospace engineering in Madras Institue of Technology.

Career as a Sceintist

1960 Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.

He staterd his Career by designing a small hovercraft. 1969 Kalam was transferred to the Indian space Research Organisation(ISRO).

Presidency

Kalam served as the 11th President of India. He won the 2002 Presidential election.

Awards & Honour

1981 - Award Padma BHushan

1990 - Award Padma BHushan

1997 - Award Bharat Ratna

Death

On 27 july 2015(aged 83) Shillong,Meghalaya,India
